    Project Idea

    This project idea is based on my personal goal where all children have access to good education and access to good quality information. As we all have been told that the pathways to economic success is the undertaking of additional years of education. The better educated are usually characterised by lower rates of unemployment, higher earnings and more prestigious occupational standing. The goal is to bring more books online, so that all digital learners have access to a great digital library on par with a major metropolitan public library system.

    To begin with I propose to create a project that targets local schools then hopefully go global. In the UNESCO website (http://www.unesco.org/new/en/gefi/priorities/quality-of-learning) they point out that there are 57 million children out of school and 617 million youth worldwide lack the basic mathematics and literacy skills. Ben Edwards and Jennifer Baxter article (2014) examined access to educational services in the disadvantage region in Australia. They point out that Australian children in regional areas have poorer learning outcomes.

    My proposal is to create a free access to online library for secondary and primary schools and students. This library is like the way university online libraries works. The difference is that content will be age appropriate and will have a look of a search engine.

    This Library differ from the Internet, because the Internet is primarily comprised of materials that are unpublished and are not vetted by anyone. By contrast, libraries are built using credible, chosen materials that have been selected for inclusion by a professional. The materials may be available under open access, but the content is controlled by the organization partnering with Libraries and government education. Students will have 10 days free access to digital books, audios, pictures and videos. This online library will be operating from within the school’s website or wider network internet. It will require setting up a hosting environment that is approved by the Department of Education. Clear guidelines about site design and content that is integrated with the school’s current online environment or Learning Management System.

    Currently it is very difficult for student to do any proper research on the internet, because they are constantly having to filter advertisements and fake or incorrect information. The current filter that is used in government school doesn’t work. When students search for information online sometimes good websites are blocked and bad ones are not. In my many years of teaching experience I still struggle to monitor and guide students while they research online. Often, I have directed students to websites that I have vetted, but because student access are restricted students are then left with information that I can’t be sure its correct and safe. The current government school online is not able to distinguish between good and bad online content. There are so many local libraries that offer online access to books, it would be great be able to have access to them in one site.

    It is important to work with libraries, authors, and publishers to build a collaborative digital collection accessible to any children in the country.
